Research

Mitochondrial fission & human neurogenesis

Dynamin-Related Protein 1 (DRP1) is the primary effector of mitochondrial and peroxisomal fission. Mutations in DRP1 cause EMPF1 — a rare epileptic encephalopathy characterized by impaired neurogenesis and severe neurodevelopmental delay. Despite its essential role, the mechanisms by which organelle fission contributes to metabolic signaling and cell fate determination during human neurogenesis remain poorly understood.

My doctoral research in the Gama Lab at Vanderbilt interrogates this critical question by characterizing how DRP1 activity shapes metabolic signaling and early human neurogenesis. I address these questions using human induced pluripotent stem cells (hiPSCs) carrying DRP1 mutations — generated via CRISPR/Cas9, or by reprogramming fibroblasts from affected individuals — to derive neural progenitors and neurons as human cellular disease models.

DRP1 & Mitochondrial Fission

Characterizing how DRP1 GTPase activity regulates mitochondrial morphology, metabolic output, and peroxisomal dynamics in neural progenitor cells during differentiation.

hiPSC Disease Modeling

Engineering patient-derived iPSC lines with CRISPR/Cas9 to introduce DRP1 mutations, then differentiating into cortical neural progenitors and neurons to study the cellular basis of EMPF1.

Metabolic Signaling

Investigating how impaired fission disrupts acetyl-CoA metabolism and downstream signaling during early neurogenesis.
